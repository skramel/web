Wesleyan Vertical Water Tunnel (2016)

Funded by the Army Research Office in collaboration with Cornell University.

Construction of a 14 ft. tall, vertical water tunnel to investigate the dynamics of sedimenting particles. The results of these measurements are to enhance the understanding of fundamental processes appearing in cloud microphysics, optical phenomena and remote sensing. (Google "Sundog" and look at the beautiful consequence of ice crystals in the atmosphere!)

Chiral Dipoles (2014)

Funded by the National Science Foundation in collaboration with TU/Eindhoven, Netherlands.

The goal of this project was to design a custom particle whose specific design would couple to isotropic turbulence and respond with a preferential rotation. As surprising as it might sound to find preferential rotation in something as seemingly random as isotropic turbulence, clever particle design can take advantage of the underlying structures in turbulence.

Scanning Laser Particle Tracking (2011)

Funded by the National Science Foundation.

Developed new optical imaging system to resolve the smallest scales of turbulence and the velocity field around fibers [published JFM, 2015]. This work revealed new insights into the dynamics of fibers and the fundamental processes of turbulence.
